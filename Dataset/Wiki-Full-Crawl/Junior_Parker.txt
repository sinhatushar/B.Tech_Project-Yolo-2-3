Herman "Junior" Parker (March 27, 1932  November 18, 1971) was an American Memphis blues singer and musician. He is best remembered for his unique voice, which has been described as "honeyed" and "velvet-smooth".  One music journalist noted, "For years Junior Parker deserted downhome harmonica blues for uptown blues-soul music".
In 2001, he was inducted into the Blues Hall of Fame.
