Mikhail Vasilyevich Lomonosov (; Russian:  ()  , IPA: [mxil vsiljvt  lmnosf] (listen); November 19 [O.S. November 8] 1711  April 15 [O.S. April 4] 1765) was a Russian polymath, scientist and writer, who made important contributions to literature, education, and science. Among his discoveries were the atmosphere of Venus and the law of conservation of mass in chemical reactions. His spheres of science were natural science, chemistry, physics, mineralogy, history, art, philology, optical devices and others. Lomonosov was also a poet and influenced the formation of the modern Russian literary language.
