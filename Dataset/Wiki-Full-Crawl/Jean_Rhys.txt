Jean Rhys,  (; born Ella Gwendolyn Rees Williams (24 August 1890  14 May 1979), was a mid-20th-century novelist who was born and grew up in the Caribbean island of Dominica. From the age of 16, she was mainly resident in England, where she was sent for her education. She is best known for her novel Wide Sargasso Sea (1966), written as a prequel to Charlotte Bront's Jane Eyre. In 1978 she was awarded the Order of the British Empire for her writing.
